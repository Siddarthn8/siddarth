About Me

I have always been fascinated by manufacturing industries, which led me to pursue Mechanical Engineering for my undergraduate education. During my studies, I encountered the risks and hazards associated with working on machinery. Through extensive research aimed at addressing these issues, I discovered Robotics as a potential solution to replace human involvement in hazardous situations. This revelation significantly piqued my interest in Robotics.

My expertise lies in control systems, robot kinematics and dynamics, and AI techniques like neural networks and computer vision enable robots to adapt to their environment and navigate accurately. I excel in path planning, manipulation, and human-robot interaction, leveraging the Robot Operating System (ROS) for scalable development, perception, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), motion planning. These areas have become the foundation of my academic and professional endeavors, allowing me to contribute effectively to innovative projects and advancements in robotics and automation.
